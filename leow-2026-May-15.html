📖 How This Works

Based on Dan John and Pavel Tsatsouline's formulas for older, advanced athletes.
Weights and reps are guidelines, not rules. Your body decides what's right.

The RPE Approach (Rate of Perceived Exertion)

How hard something feels, not what the scale says.

DayTarget FeelWhat This Means
Day A (Barbell)HardYou want to feel a difficult push. 5set3rep should feel challenging by set 4-5.
Day B (KB EMOM)ModerateYou're building. 7-10 reps per minute should feel achievable but not easy.
Day C (Bodyweight + KB)HardHard bodyweight sets, then a short KB burn. Push through it.
Day D (Bodyweight Light)LightThis should feel light. Recovery day. Move well, not hard.

The Simple Rule

Heavy days (A, C): You want to feel the work.

Light days (D): You want to feel like you could do more.

If Day A feels too easy, add weight.

If Day A feels brutal, take weight off.

We're not following a rigid formula. We're building a sustainable practice that adapts to how you feel. This is why you establish weights in Week 1 - so you have a baseline to compare against.

📅 Week 1: Establish Your Weights

Day A - Establish Barbell Weights

Barbell 60-75 min

WARM-UP:
  5-10 min light cardio (bike, row, jumping jacks)
  3-5 warm-up sets building to working weight

MAIN:
  Back Squat 5set3rep
    - Start light, add weight each set
    - Find a weight where 5set3rep feels HARD but doable
    - This is your baseline weight

  Overhead Press 5set3rep
    - Same approach
    - Start lighter than you think

FINISHER:
  Pushups: 3-4 sets, stop 3 reps before failure
  Pullups/Ring Rows: 2 sets, 5-8 reps each

What does "stop 3 reps before failure" mean?

If you can do 20 pushups max, do 17 per set (20 - 3 = 17).

If you can do 8 pullups max, do 5 per set (8 - 3 = 5).

Day B - Establish KB Reps

KB EMOM 30-40 min

WARM-UP:
  5 min light cardio + few practice swings

MAIN:
  20 min EMOM @ 7-10 swings per minute
  
  Start at 7 reps per minute.
  Increase week over week at a comfortable pace up to 10 per minute.
  This takes months to build up to.
  
  Option A: 2-hand swings every minute
  Option B: Right hand min 1, Left hand min 2 (alternate)
  
  Total: 140-200 swings

You can rest the remaining seconds each minute.

Day C - Establish Bodyweight Reps

Bodyweight + KB HIIT 45-60 min

WARM-UP:
  5 min light cardio

PART 1: BODYWEIGHT (20-25 min)
  Pullups/Ring Rows: 4 sets, stop 3 reps before failure
  Pushups: 4 sets, stop 3 reps before failure

PART 2: KB HIIT
  5 min EMOM @ 15-20 rpm
    - Start at 15 swings per minute
    - Increase week over week at a comfortable pace up to 20
    - All 20 swings in a row, then rest the minute
    - You might not get much recovery time, that's the point
    - Once 20 is easy, upgrade to heavier bell
    - When you upgrade bell size, go back to 15 reps per minute
    - This progression takes months or years

Day D - Establish Light Bodyweight

Bodyweight Light 30-40 min

WARM-UP:

MAIN:

  Pushups: 5 sets, stop 3 reps before failure

📚 KB Long-Term Progression

KB EMOM

Build from 7 to 10 reps per minute, week over week.
This takes months.

Once at 10/min and it feels easy, upgrade to heavier bell.
When you upgrade bell size, go back to 7 reps per minute.

Trained adults usually only go up in KB weight once or twice over 5 years.
Ballistics don't require constant weight jumps.

KB HIIT

Start at 15 reps per minute.
Increase week over week at a comfortable pace up to 20.

Once 20 is easy, upgrade to heavier bell.
When you upgrade bell size, go back to 15 reps per minute.

This progression takes months or years.

📚 Pullup Progression

Can't do pullups?
  1. Ring Rows (start here)
  2. Incline Pullups (feet on bench/chair)
  3. Strict Pullups (goal)

Each level: 4 sets, stop 3 reps before failure
Progress when you can do 4 sets of 8+ reps easily.

⚠️ When to Back Off

Deload when YOU need to, not on a schedule. Add an extra rest day or reduce volume if:

  • Sleep quality drops
  • Joint pain that doesn't resolve
  • The weights feel heavy when they shouldn't
  • Activity performance suffers

The Simple Protocol: Establish weights → Keep going → Add weight when easy → Back off when heavy.
